Thank you for your interest in our work.

Heald Lab has been around for a while...

And as cell biologists tend to do, we generate a lot of imaging data. As we were going through the many hard drives of our images, we wondered - how could we make it #open to the general research community? How not to let it disappear as so much primary data do?

And then, there were the daily concerns many of us face...

Sometimes it is not really obvious, whether the treatment we apply results in a phenotype.

Sometimes it is not really obvious, what to measure to even detect it.

Sometimes we are worried that we see, what we want to see but is not there. Or that we are missing, what is.

Out of all these considerations, this project was born.

With this publication we would like to share the thousands of images we collected over the past ~5 years, as well as propose a solution, which allows to quantify multiple characteristics about the captured spindle objects. We also suggest some analysis approaches, to help narrow down potential metrics (features/variables) of interest. We are basically presenting here a biological big (or medium) data-driven hypothesis generator, an exploratory one, which should help launch cell biologists onto new research tracks.
